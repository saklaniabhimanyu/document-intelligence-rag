Document Intelligence System — Internal Engineering Note

1
Section 1: Overview

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) combines a retriever and a generator. The
retriever finds relevant passages from a document collection using vector
similarity search. The generator, typically a large language model, then
produces an answer conditioned on the retrieved passages rather than relying
purely on parameters learned during training.

This grounding step is the key difference between RAG and standard LLM chat.
A standard chat model can only answer from what it memorized during training,
which means it will hallucinate confidently when asked about documents it has
never seen, such as an internal company policy or a freshly published report.

2
Section 2: Why Chunking Matters

Documents are split into chunks before embedding because embedding models
have a limited context window and because retrieval works best when each
vector represents one coherent idea rather than an entire multi-topic
document. A chunk size of around 500 characters with 50 characters of overlap
is a reasonable starting point for prose-heavy documents; technical manuals
with short, dense paragraphs sometimes benefit from smaller chunks.

Overlap exists so that a sentence split across the boundary of two chunks
still appears in full inside at least one chunk, which prevents losing
context at the seam.

3
Section 3: Evaluation

Faithfulness measures whether the generated answer is actually supported by
the retrieved context, rather than introducing claims that the context does
not contain. Answer relevancy measures whether the answer actually addresses
the question that was asked. Context precision measures whether the
retrieved chunks were the right ones, and context recall measures whether
all the necessary information was retrieved at all.
